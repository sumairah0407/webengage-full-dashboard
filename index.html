◈
Strategic Intelligence — CLM Analysis
Data-driven insights across all clusters
⚠ Conversion Event Contamination — Cross-Campaign Comparisons Are Invalid
Across 24 campaigns, "conversion" measures 4 different things: click (CITN, AssetX), open (Allocate WA, Advance Tax, AUM), specific in-app event (SIF Invest, portfolio fetch), and session start (Login OTP). Rolling these into a single "conversion rate" column creates a category error. Allocate's 59% conversion isn't comparable to CITN's 8% — they're measuring different funnel stages entirely. Recommendation: standardise a unified intent event (e.g. "app session >60s" or "feature page visit") as the primary cross-campaign conversion signal going forward.
◆ Audience Quality Is The Dominant Performance Variable — 59x Range
Open rate ranges from 59% (Allocate clients) to 1% (meta static list) — a 59x spread. This is not a content quality problem; it's a list quality problem. The signal is clear: relationship depth and recency of opt-in predict engagement better than any content variable. Meta static list (cold, unreachable leads) produced 98% delivery but 1% open — the phone is ringing but no one's picking up. The spend on those sends is effectively wasted. Invest in opt-in health audits and suppress stale lists after 90 days of no activity.
↑ AOL v2: The Click–Conversion Inversion Validates Intent-Based Routing
v2 cut click rate from 25% → 12% while lifting portfolio conversion from 1% → 24%. This counterintuitive result is the hallmark of a well-designed intent funnel: separate deeplinks for MF vs stock fetch removed ambiguous clicks and routed users directly to the relevant action. In CLM, a lower CTR with a higher conversion rate is always preferable — it means you've stopped counting curiosity as intent. The 24% stock fetch rate is a genuine leading indicator of conversion readiness for these leads.
⊞ Brand CLM: 5.5% Delivery Requires Immediate Diagnosis
With 4,386 entries and only 242 delivered (5.5% delivery rate), Brand CLM has a structural problem — not a messaging problem. At this delivery rate, 94.5% of the journey's communication attempts are failing before the user sees a single word. Root causes to investigate in priority order: (1) WhatsApp opt-in rate for brand UTM leads — likely very low if leads came from paid media without explicit WA opt-in; (2) DND & frequency capping overlap with other concurrent journeys; (3) phone number quality from UTM capture forms. Fix delivery before optimising content.
⚡ Event-Triggered > Scheduled: Union Budget Proves Real-World Urgency Is Irreplicable
Union Budget achieved 54% open rate — the second highest in the portfolio — on a cold/warm prospect audience that typically delivers 17–22%. The single variable: real-world event urgency. The human brain processes irreversibility (budget announced, market moving now) differently from scheduled drip content. This is a reproducible playbook — identify 6–8 annual financial events (RBI policy, Union Budget, LTCG changes, NFO closures, Nifty milestones) and build standing templates with pre-approved audience segments so you can deploy within 2 hours of the event.
⊛ SIF Multi-Channel Orchestration: Attribution Gap Understates True Impact
SIF WA drip ran simultaneously with email drip and push — the same user likely received all three. With no cross-channel deduplication in WebEngage, conversions are being attributed to whichever channel the user last engaged with. The actual journey-level conversion was 10% from the drip journey alone, but the individual WA and email conversion rates are in the 0.03–1% range. The multi-touch contribution model needs to be set up in WebEngage to accurately credit the awareness function of WA and the intent-capture function of push. Currently you're measuring channel performance, not campaign performance.
↗ Global Webinar: 51% Read / 2.36% Click — Classic Awareness-to-Action Friction
The 51% read rate proves the content is compelling — HNI/UHNI prospects are reading WA messages about global investments. The drop from 51% read to 2.36% click is a CTA design problem, not an audience problem. For this cohort, friction at the link level is disproportionately high: they read on WhatsApp but won't leave to a browser without a strong reason. Recommendation: (1) move registration to a WhatsApp-native RSVP using a quick reply button rather than a link, (2) include a specific promise ("You'll see a live allocation into Japanese equities"), (3) A/B test time-of-send — this audience is likely checking WA at 8–10pm weekdays based on SIF timing learnings.
◉ Allocate WA Delivery Ceiling at 61% — Meta Utility Classification Is Throttling High-Value Comms
The Allocate monthly update is your highest-quality communication (59% open, direct client utility) yet delivery is capped at 61% due to Meta's utility message classification. The root cause is confirmed — punctuation and exclamation marks trigger a "marketing" classification that applies frequency capping. Immediate fix: run the message template through WhatsApp's policy checker before submission, remove all punctuation beyond periods and commas, and test as a transactional template. Structural fix: explore "service" message classification for portfolio update content — this bypasses marketing windows entirely and can deliver to opted-out users.
◈ Audience Tier Framework — Predictive Engagement Segmentation
Based on observed open rate patterns, your reachable database has at least 4 distinct engagement tiers:

Tier 1 (50–60% open): Allocate clients, event-triggered prospects — highest intent, known relationship
Tier 2 (30–40% open): Warm prospects with recent interaction (Union Budget, Press release, SIF drip)
Tier 3 (15–25% open): AOL leads, product-informed prospects — mid funnel
Tier 4 (<5% open): Cold/stale lists — meta UTM leads, unreachable static lists

Apply this tier as a segmentation attribute in WebEngage. Use RFM scoring on engagement history (opens, clicks, app sessions) to auto-classify users and route them into appropriate journey intensity.
◆ Conversion Event Upgrade Roadmap — From Proxy to Intent
Currently, most campaigns use proxy metrics (opens, clicks) as conversion goals. To measure true CLM impact, transition to intent-level events in this sequence:

Phase 1 (immediate): Standardise "portfolio imported" and "feature page visited" as primary conversion events across all nurture journeys
Phase 2 (next quarter): Connect WebEngage journey completions to STORM lead stage transitions — measure journey → stage upgrade rate
Phase 3: Build a probabilistic conversion model: given a user's engagement sequence (WA read → app open → portfolio fetch), estimate the probability of RM conversion within 30/60/90 days. This directly ties your CLM work to AUM growth attribution.
